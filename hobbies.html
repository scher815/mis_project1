Hockey
More Than Just a Game
My biggest passion is hockey. Hockey has molded my personality and given me priceless life lessons, whether I'm playing the sport I love on the ice or watching it from the stands. Few other sports can rival the sport's combination of athleticism, strategy, and teamwork.

What I Love

Hockey is unmatched due to its pace, intensity, and collaboration requirements. Concentration, communication, and faith in your teammates are essential for every shift. On the ice, it is immensely satisfying to feel that you are a part of something greater than yourself, working toward a shared objective.

Personal Stories

Since I could walk, I've been in skates. Playoff games, out-of-town tournaments, and Minnesota's amazing hockey community are some of my favorite memories. My approach to problems in all facets of life has been impacted by my colleagues' camaraderie and the lessons I've gained from both wins and losses.

Skills Developed

I've learned self-control, tenacity, strategic thinking, and how to perform under stress from hockey. I've gained abilities that are directly related to my career goals, such as the ability to work well in teams, communicate well, and remain composed under pressure.

Why This Matters to Me

More than just a pastime, hockey is an essential component of who I am. It keeps me mentally and physically engaged. Hockey provides an outlet and a source of inspiration in a difficult academic setting. My success as an economics student and my future professional aspirations are directly impacted by the resilience, collaboration, and determination I acquired on the ice. I am reminded by hockey that a fulfilling life requires both passion and balance.

Computers & Technology
Fascination with Technology & Systems
I have a keen interest in how computers operate and how technology can be used to tackle challenging issues. I like learning about the technical facets of computing and finding new tools for data analysis and decision-making, from software architecture to hardware specs.

What Fascinates Me

The convergence of software and hardware, as well as the ability of computer systems to handle massive volumes of data and produce insightful results. I'm really curious about how technology helps with data science, business analytics, and economics decision-making. My curiosity and interest are maintained by the ongoing advancements in computer technology.

Personal Experience

I've spent time experimenting with different software tools for data analysis, learning how to construct and setup computers, and investigating programming principles. In addition to using my high-end gaming PC for gaming, I also use it to study about computing efficiency, performance analysis, and system optimization.

Learning Goals

I'm always trying to learn more about programming, data analysis, and the practical applications of AI and machine learning technology. In order to facilitate well-informed decision-making, I aim to close the gap between economic theory and useful computational techniques.

Why This Matters to Me

My academic and professional goals are heavily reliant on technology. In today's economy, knowing how to use technology and comprehend computers is no longer optional. My studies in economics are immediately enhanced by this pastime, which also helps me be ready for a career in data-driven industries like operations or analytics. I'm developing skills that will define my career future by continuing to use technology, not merely to satisfy a personal curiosity. I can turn economic insights into useful intelligence thanks to technology.
